Meet the professor

Prof. Giulia Rinaldi

A medical professor preparing teaching sessions from fragmented slides, notes, papers, and clinical examples.

Professor profile

Teaching context

Professor of Clinical Research Methods, teaching advanced students in translational and clinical research. The course relies on materials accumulated over multiple semesters.

Course type

Advanced teaching in clinical research methods

Teaching material

Slides, notes, papers, case examples, syllabus fragments

Need

Faster, structured lesson preparation without manual reconstruction

Teaching pain points

  • Teaching material is scattered across folders, files, and formats.
  • Preparation is difficult to delegate to assistants or junior staff.
  • Course logic is not always explicit; much of the structure exists only in the professor’s own head.
What this means in practice
Old decks

Prior-year slide logic remains mixed with newer material.

Scattered notes

Key explanations are often trapped in raw notes, not reusable teaching objects.

Difficult handover

Assistants struggle to know what is current, essential, or optional.

Without a structured teaching layer, lesson preparation remains person-dependent, difficult to reuse, and hard to hand over cleanly to assistants or future semesters.
